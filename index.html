The Business Reality of Cancer

Cancer Is the Highest-Risk Condition
Organizations Manage

It's not just another line item. Cancer creates significant financial volatility and systemic exposure. The greatest fear isn't limited to cost — it's spending $1M and still losing the employee.

Expert Insight Arrives Too Late

Traditional second opinions are episodic, manual, and slow — often 4–6 weeks. By then, treatment paths and costs are already set.

Catastrophic Financial Exposure

Represents 10–15% of total medical spend and 20–40% of catastrophic claims. Cancer shows 2–3× higher cost variability than most chronic conditions.

Wrong Decisions Cost Lives and Money

20–40% of second opinions change treatment. 30–60% refine diagnoses. Only 10–15% are confirmed without change.

First Decisions Determine Everything

The first diagnosis and treatment plan determine clinical outcomes, patient confidence, and long-term cost complexity. There's no rewinding.

Misdiagnosis drives $65M in avoidable cost per 100,000 employees. A single complex case can exceed $1M annually. Even small changes in treatment swing costs by $10K–$100K+.
