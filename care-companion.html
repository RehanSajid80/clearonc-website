Our Model

The ClearOnc Care Companion Model

Cancer is not a single event — it’s a series of inflection points. ClearOnc was built to be a care companion throughout each member’s journey, providing trusted expertise at every turning point.

Stage 1

At Diagnosis

When everything feels urgent, we confirm the foundation:

  • Is the diagnosis accurate?
  • Is the staging correct?
  • Are all recommended diagnostics complete?

Early clarity changes everything that follows.

Stage 2

During Treatment Planning

We recommend the right therapy for the member — prioritizing not only outcomes, but also patient context and preferences against real-time guidelines and subspecialty expertise.

Small adjustments at this stage can prevent unnecessary toxicity, escalation, or costly downstream reversals.

Stage 3

When Something Changes

If cancer progresses. If side effects emerge. If a new therapy becomes available.

ClearOnc re-engages, ensuring the next decision is as informed as the first.

Stage 4

Through Survivorship

For many people, the cancer journey continues long after treatment ends.

ClearOnc helps patients and families stay connected to trusted expertise through survivorship, supporting follow-up care, prevention, and long-term monitoring.

Multi-Level Value

Impact at Every Level

For Members

  • Rapid access to trusted cancer expertise — in days, not weeks
  • Clarity when facing some of the hardest medical decisions
  • Confidence as treatment plans change and evolve
  • Continued support throughout the cancer journey

Because no one should have to navigate life-changing cancer decisions alone.

For Clinical Case Managers and Navigators

  • Structured and fast record synthesis — in minutes, not hours
  • Expert-backed, referenceable recommendations
  • Real-time guideline alignment
  • Support when plans change

We reduce friction so care teams can guide members with confidence.

For Employers and Health Plans

  • Earlier expert insight — before high-cost escalation
  • Reduced treatment variability — even small changes can lead to $10K–$100K in costs per decision
  • Improved catastrophic claim predictability — cancer accounts for 20–40% of all catastrophic claims
  • Standardized and auditable oncology review

Managing the full journey — not just isolated decisions — reduces risk over time.
